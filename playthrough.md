# 10K Mobile Game: Playthrough Concept

This document outlines a sequential playthrough of a single hand of 10K, designed for a 3D mobile game interface. The sequence follows a player as they risk points to reach a winning total, capturing the progression from a promising initial roll to a risky final decision.

---

### Image 1: The Initial Roll (Establishing the Game)

The first step is the beginning of the turn. The screen shows a vibrant digital green felt surface within the mobile interface. The player has rolled all six dice. It’s a fantastic start: the dice land to show a scoring hand. There is a '5', a '1', and three '3's (a three-of-a-kind). The UI clearly indicates the **Current Score** is 0 and the **Potential Points** for this roll are 450 (100 for the '1', 50 for the '5', and 300 for the three '3's). Only the single '2' is a non-scoring die.

### Image 2: The Decisions (Setting Aside Dice)

The game now moves to the decision phase. We see the exact same layout as Image 1, confirming continuity. The player has chosen their strategy: they are keeping the three '3's and the single '1' (highlighted now by a soft glow) to secure 400 points. The single '5' (the 50-point die) is left unmarked, as the player decides not to bank it yet. They will tempt fate by rolling the remaining single '2' (now isolated) to try and increase their score. This sets up the tension for the next roll.

### Image 3: The Risk (A Disappointing Roll)

The player has pushed their luck. In Image 3, we see the results of rolling that single, isolated '2' seen in Image 1 and Image 2. The four scoring dice (1, 3, 3, 3) from the previous phase are safely displayed in a dedicated sidebar rack on the left, visual confirmation they are 'locked in' for 400 points. However, the one remaining die has been re-rolled and has landed as a '2' again. The lighting has turned from green to a subtle, warm amber, signaling bad news. This roll (the single '2') scores zero points. The player is now at a critical crossroads.

### Image 4: The Final Outcome (Bank or Bust)

The sequence concludes with the moment of truth. Following the non-scoring roll in Image 3, the player decides to cut their losses and **Bank** the points they have accumulated. The scene uses the established 3D visual continuity: the green felt surface remains the background. The four scoring dice (1, 3, 3, 3) move center stage and are highlighted by a secure, glowing energy field as they physically merge into the interface. A prominent digital overlay displays the final result in bold, gold text: **+400**. The "BANK" button is depressed and glowing green. The player has successfully locked in their score for the round, avoiding a complete 'bust.'
